Certificate Summary

As my journey with the New Media Institute at the University of Georgia comes to an end, I’ve come to realize how much I’ve truly learned from this program. Not only have I expanded my coding and development skills in ways that I otherwise wouldn’t have had the chance to, this experience has also exposed me to design and marketing techniques, and given me real world client experience. These courses and the people in them have led me to realize my other passions, such as UI/UX design and Web Development. The New Media Institute has taught me to be confident in exploring my abilities and expanding my skills, as well as working with other people of different majors and backgrounds to create a cohesive product.

Digital Brown Bag

Admittedly, when I finally came around to taking the Digital Brown Bag course my final semester at UGA, I wasn’t expecting to be impacted as much as I was. This course exposed me to so many different people who have taken so many different paths in life. From social media management start-ups to business and marketing lawyers, I never knew how much I would learn in this course. These guest speakers came prepared and inspired, which consequently inspired me to take various pages from their books. I learned about job applications, moving to a new city, and even personal finance management. These applicable skills are perhaps some of the least expected, yet most valuable lectures I’ve attended during my four years at this university.

Rich New Media Production

The Rich New Media course was the primary reason I decided to pursue the New Media Certificate. While there is a course offered in the Computer Science Department that focuses on Android Development, I was very curious as to the development process of iOS apps and what the course would focus on. Under the instruction of my professor, Emuel Aldridge, I learned so much about iOS app development, brainstorming ideas for projects, and finding new resources online about skills that I can add to my applications.
Not only did I learn about the original Swift language, about halfway through the course we made the switch to SwiftUI, the new language that had just been released by Apple. This was not simply a new experience for the students, but for Professor Aldridge as well. During the latter half of the course, I greatly admired his ability to learn about this brand new language on the fly, and how he encouraged us to do the same. Through this, I learned not only the hard skill of SwiftUI, but the soft skill of being flexible and engaged in every part of your development journey.

Web Programming

Web Programming was one of the most interesting and challenging courses I took at the University of Georgia. Not only did we focus on aesthetics of a website, we also took the time to explore JSON parsing, SQL queries, and some Javascript and JQuery skills. This class was a little bit of everything all in one course, and while it was challenging, it was also highly rewarding. I enjoyed every project I participated in during this course, and hope to find the abilities that I learned highly useful in my future career.

New Media Capstone

The Capstone class in the New Media Institute is by far one of the most challenging courses I have experienced. I deeply enjoyed working with my amazing team on our project, and was able to learn so many new skills in relation to web application development. Working directly with a client was also an eye-opening experience, with the pressure to succeed on a semester time schedule driving me to push myself and what I could do in that time. Not only did I thoroughly enjoy my individual part of the assignment, but also collaborating with my teammates. In the Computer Science department, we often don’t get the chance to work with other majors, and this class allowed me the chance to expose myself to so many different kinds of people. I look forward to using the communication skills I learned by working with this diverse team in the future.
